# Cafe Chain Sales Analysis Report
January 1 – June 30, 2023
## Project Overview

This project analyzes a large dataset containing more than 100,000 transaction records from a cafe chain operating across multiple locations during the period from January 1 to June 30, 2023.

The workbook consists of three worksheets:

Data

 * transaction_id – unique transaction identifier
 * transaction_date – transaction date
 * transaction_time – transaction time
 * transaction_qty – quantity of items purchased
 * store_id – unique cafe identifier
 * store_location – cafe location
 * product_id – product identifier
 * unit_price – price per item
 * product_category – product category
 * product_type – product type

Product Description

 * product_id – unique product identifier
 * product_detail – detailed product description

The objective of the analysis was to prepare the dataset for reporting, identify low-selling products, analyze sales trends and create management reports for decision-making.
## Data Preparation

Before performing the analysis the dataset underwent a comprehensive cleaning and preprocessing process.

The following steps were completed:

 * Visual inspection of the dataset to identify potential issues.
 * Removal of duplicate and empty records (3,000 duplicate rows were removed).
 * Removal of unnecessary spaces from 11,468 cells.
 * Correction of data formats in 40,776 rows.
 * Sorting the dataset by transaction_id.
 * Applying filters to detect missing values and anomalous records.
 * Reviewing data through  AutoCalculate tools to identify inconsistencies.

After completing these steps the dataset was validated and prepared for further analysis.

## Analysis of Low-Selling Products
Business Request

The cafe manager requested an analysis of the lowest-selling products in order to evaluate whether some items should be removed from the menu.

Methodology

To identify low-performing products Excel functions including VLOOKUP and SUMIF were used. A ranking of the ten lowest-selling products was generated based on total sales volume.

## Comparison of Low-Selling Products Across Cafes
Business Request

The manager wanted to determine whether the same products perform poorly across all cafes.

Methodology

A pivot table named "Low-Selling Products" was created to compare product sales across all cafe locations. Products were sorted in ascending order by sales volume.

<img width="1801" height="652" alt="image4" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/cd1ed7e6-4dfe-481d-9572-226558d7d4ed" />

Findings

Three products consistently appeared among the lowest-selling items in every cafe:

* Dark Chocolate
* Spiced Masala Tea Eye Opener
* Organic Guatemala

These products ranked among the weakest performers across all locations indicating that poor sales are not limited to a specific cafe.

Conclusion

The consistently low sales of these products suggest a chain-wide demand issue rather than a location-specific problem.

## Monthly Sales Trend Analysis
Business Request

The manager wanted to verify whether low sales were caused by products being recently introduced to the menu.

Methodology

A pivot table named "Sales Trends" was created to analyze monthly sales performance. Monthly sales data was reviewed for each low-selling product.

<img width="1310" height="623" alt="image3" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/bad818f8-b3b5-4430-95bf-0fa82004074f" />

Findings

The analysis showed that the products identified as low-selling were available throughout the entire reporting period. None of the products were recent additions to the menu.

Conclusion

Low sales are not related to product launch timing. These products consistently generated weak sales month after month across the entire six-month period.

## Revenue and Sales Report
Business Request

The task was to prepare a report for the CEO showing revenue and sales volume by cafe, month and product category.

Methodology

A pivot table named "Revenue & Sales Report" was created.

The report provides:

* Revenue by cafe
* Sales quantity by cafe
* Monthly performance
* Product category breakdown

Findings
* Revenue distribution across cafes is relatively balanced.
* No single cafe significantly outperformed or underperformed the others.
* Product categories contribute differently to total revenue.
* Chocolate Bar products generated the lowest revenue among all categories analyzed.
  
Conclusion

The cafe chain demonstrates stable revenue distribution across locations. However several low-performing products consistently underperform across all cafes and may require further evaluation, promotional support, menu redesign or removal from the product assortment.

## Key Findings
* More than 3,000 duplicate records were removed during data cleaning.
* Data quality issues were successfully resolved before analysis.
* Low-selling products were identified using transaction-level sales data.
* Dark Chocolate, Spiced Masala Tea Eye Opener and Organic Guatemala consistently ranked among the worst-selling products in every cafe.
* These products have been available throughout the reporting period and were not recently introduced.
* Revenue distribution across cafes is relatively even.
* Chocolate Bar products generated the lowest revenue contribution.
## Recommendations
* Review the pricing, placement and marketing of consistently low-selling products.
* Consider removing products that underperform across all cafe locations.
* Monitor product performance monthly to identify declining trends earlier.
* Continue using category-level revenue analysis to optimize the product portfolio.
* Conduct customer feedback research before making final menu changes.
